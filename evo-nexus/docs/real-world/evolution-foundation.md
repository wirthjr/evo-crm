# Case Study: Evolution Foundation

## The Company

[Evolution Foundation](https://evolutionfoundation.com.br) builds open source WhatsApp and messaging integration tools. Their main product, [Evolution API](https://github.com/EvolutionAPI/evolution-api), powers thousands of messaging workflows worldwide. The company has a 7,000+ member community across Discord and WhatsApp, multiple active repositories on GitHub, Stripe billing, and a growing open source user base.

The CEO runs operations, development coordination, community management, financial oversight, social media, and strategy — with a small distributed team.

## The Setup

Evolution Foundation runs EvoNexus with all 17 agents active:

- **Clawdia** — daily ops, email, calendar, task management
- **Flux** — Stripe revenue tracking, ERP integration (Omie), financial reports
- **Atlas** — GitHub PRs, Linear issues, sprint coordination
- **Pulse** — Discord and WhatsApp community monitoring
- **Pixel** — YouTube, Instagram, LinkedIn analytics and content
- **Sage** — OKRs, strategy digests, competitive analysis
- **Nex** — sales pipeline and proposals
- **Mentor** — Evo Academy course planning
- **Lumen** — knowledge retention, spaced repetition, fact capture
- **Kai** — personal health and habit tracking
- **Oracle** — workspace knowledge, docs, configuration
- **Mako** — marketing campaigns, SEO, brand reviews
- **Aria** — HR, recruiting pipeline, onboarding
- **Zara** — customer success, ticket triage, escalation
- **Lex** — legal, contracts, compliance checks
- **Nova** — product specs, roadmaps, metrics
- **Dex** — data analysis, dashboards, SQL queries

Connected integrations: Google Calendar, Gmail, GitHub, Linear, Discord, Telegram, Stripe, Omie, Fathom, Todoist, YouTube, Instagram, LinkedIn, and the Evolution Licensing API.

## A Typical Day

The scheduler runs 11+ daily routines from early morning to late evening:

### Morning Block (6:50 AM - 7:15 AM)

| Time | Routine | What happens |
|------|---------|-------------|
| 6:50 | Review Todoist | Clawdia organizes and prioritizes tasks, flags overdue items |
| 7:00 | Morning Briefing | Calendar review, email summary, task priorities, meeting prep |
| 7:15 | Email Triage | Classify inbox by urgency, draft replies for high-priority messages |

By 7:30 AM, the CEO opens a single morning report and knows exactly what needs attention.

### Throughout the Day

Every 30 minutes, meetings are synced from Fathom — transcripts are processed, action items extracted, and tasks created automatically.

### Evening Block (6:00 PM - 9:30 PM)

| Time | Routine | What happens |
|------|---------|-------------|
| 6:00 PM | Social Analytics | Cross-platform report: YouTube, Instagram, LinkedIn engagement |
| 6:30 PM | Licensing Daily | Open source telemetry: active instances, geo distribution, versions |
| 7:00 PM | Financial Pulse | Stripe MRR, charges, churn + Omie receivables/payables |
| 8:00 PM | Community Pulse | Discord and WhatsApp activity, sentiment, support questions |
| 8:15 PM | FAQ Sync | Update community FAQ from recurring questions |
| 9:00 PM | End of Day | Consolidate everything into a daily log |
| 9:15 PM | Memory Sync | Update persistent memory with today's learnings |
| 9:30 PM | Dashboard | Generate unified 360-degree HTML dashboard |

## Weekly Cadence

Every Friday, a deeper analysis cycle runs:

- **Financial Weekly** — week-over-week revenue, expenses, cash flow projection
- **Licensing Weekly** — open source growth trends, version adoption curves
- **Weekly Review** — consolidated review of the entire week
- **Social Analytics Weekly** — content performance, audience growth
- **Trends Analysis** — cross-domain pattern detection (community + finance + product)
- **Strategy Digest** — strategic insights from all data sources

Monday/Wednesday/Friday:
- **Linear Review** — sprint progress, blockers, issues in review
- **GitHub Review** — open PRs, community contributions, release status

Sunday:
- **Health Check-in** — personal wellness tracking via Kai

## Monthly Close

On the 1st of each month:
- **Monthly Close Kickoff** — Flux initiates the financial close process with checklists and P&L
- **Community Monthly** — Pulse generates a full monthly community report with MAM and growth trends
- **Licensing Monthly** — Atlas produces a 30-day open source growth analysis
- **Social Analytics Monthly** — Pixel consolidates monthly cross-platform performance

## Results

After running EvoNexus for daily operations:

- **30+ hours saved per week** on routine operational tasks
- **Zero missed follow-ups** — every meeting action item becomes a tracked task
- **Real-time financial health** — no more waiting for monthly reports to spot problems
- **Community issues caught early** — sentiment shifts and recurring questions surface in hours, not weeks
- **Consistent reporting** — every day produces the same structured outputs, regardless of how busy the day was

The key insight: the value isn't in any single routine. It's in the compounding effect of having every domain monitored, every day, with memory that carries forward. Problems don't slip through cracks when 17 agents are watching their domains on schedule.
